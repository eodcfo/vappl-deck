Chapter 02

The Opportunity

India's urban middle class is ready to go out — and they want to do something.

For decades, recreation in India meant the rare occasion — the annual holiday, the once-a-year theme park, the special outing saved for a reason. What has changed is aspiration. India's per capita income has grown 2.5× in a decade. Tier 2 and 3 cities have metro connectivity, expressways, and disposable income that didn't exist a generation ago. A growing middle class has moved from surviving well to living well — and living well means doing things, not just buying things. COVID was a catalyst for what was already in motion. It didn't create the shift. It made an entire generation conscious of it faster.

The evidence is in how Indians now spend their time. The way festivals are celebrated has changed — Navratri 2024 generated ₹50,000 Cr nationally in nine days, not from religious offerings but from ticketed garba nights and carnival infrastructure (CAIT, 2024). India hosted 30,687 live events across 319 cities in 2024, rising to 34,000 in 2025. Over 70% of young urban Indians attended at least one ticketed live event during the year. Indians are paying to do things — actively, repeatedly, and at scale. The shift from passive to active is measurable, durable, and still early.

02.1

The formats that exist were built for a different era

→

Destination parks · ₹1,200+

A full day. A car. A calendar slot. Wonderla. Imagica. Built for the occasion, not the habit.

→

Malls & multiplexes

Passive. Consumptive. Interchangeable. The thing the 18–35 cohort is moving away from.

→

Indoor Amusement Centres · ₹500–800

Growing fast — 67% capacity expansion by 2028 (JLL) — but structurally locked into the premium segment. IACs anchor inside malls and high-footfall retail developments, paying commercial rents that demand premium ticket prices to survive. They serve the mall-going customer well. But they can't serve the neighbourhood: high rental floors, footfall ceilings, and no outdoor scale. Delhi NCR's 68 IAC centres average roughly 13,000 sq ft each — enough for a few hundred visitors at a time, not a city.

→

Public parks

Free, but inert. No structured activity. No reason to return next week.

The neighbourhood adventure park — urban, accessible, active, priced for frequency — does not exist at scale in India. Every concert, every garba night, every carnival proves the appetite is there. The gap is every other day.

E-O-D was built for exactly that.

The Market

India's recreation spend, sized from the bottom up.

India's amusement park and indoor entertainment industry stands at approximately ₹11,500 Cr, growing at 15% annually toward ₹25,000 Cr by 2030 — figures from IAAPI, the Indian Association of Amusement Parks and Industries, the sector's own trade body (2024). JLL's September 2024 report projects India's indoor amusement centre space to expand 67% by 2028, from 6.6 mn sq ft to 11 mn sq ft across 500+ centres in 83 cities. The organized live events sector adds a further ₹20,861 Cr (2024). Gen Z now drives 43% of India's total consumer spending (BCG/Snap, 2024) and is set to command 45% of the travel and tourism market by FY2030 at nearly twice the category average (Redseer, 2026). The top-down is validated — but the bottom-up is what matters.

The Answer

We stopped selling. We started delivering. Then we rebuilt sales the right way.

In 2019, E-O-D made a decision that went against every instinct in the amusement park industry. We dismantled our group sales team — six to eight people chasing corporate outings, school trips, birthday packages. Instead we asked a simpler question: what does the person who walks in off the street actually need?

₹299
The combo ticket · founding price, 2019
One price. No menu of activity charges. No upsells. Come in, do everything, have a great afternoon.

We invested what we saved into operations, safety, and experience. We let the product do the selling. Then we rebuilt — smarter.

04.1

Three streams. Three distribution models. Zero bloated fixed cost.

01 · Walk-in

Content + UGC, zero sales cost

Guests discover E-O-D through Instagram, Google, and word-of-mouth — powered by an active content team and organic UGC posted by guests after every visit. No dedicated walk-in sales function. The product and the content do the selling. Highest-volume, lowest cost-of-acquisition of any stream.

02 · Schools & Groups

Travel agent network + off-site salesperson

10–20 agents in 2022. 150+ active agents by FY25-26. Commission built into the package — agents earn, we earn, no fixed overhead. One variable-pay off-site salesperson closes direct school enquiries and corporate group bookings. School distribution is EMV-primary today; EAC and DME activation follows as those parks scale.

03 · Digital

Online booking + Annual Pass

Direct booking via eodparks.com and BookMyShow. Annual Pass for loyal recurring visitors. Owned channel, growing as a structured recurring stream.

Serve the walk-in guest obsessively. Price for frequency. Generate leads digitally. Only pay for sales that close.

The Journey

One park. One city. One bet on the neighbourhood.

2015 · Gates open

EMV launches at Sanjay Lake, Mayur Vihar

Government concession. 6.3 acres. A park designed for groups — travel agents, corporates, schools, birthdays.

2019 · The Pivot

Stop chasing groups

₹299 combo ticket launches. The entire sales team is dismantled. We start obsessing over the walk-in guest. Every Other Day stops being a tagline and becomes an operating philosophy.

2020–21 · COVID

Revenue collapses. The team holds.

The park stays closed. We wait.

2022 · First mover

Open the day the lockdown lifted

While destination parks worked through maintenance backlogs and indoor entertainment venues fought landlord disputes, E-O-D's gates opened. No backlog. No legal battle. No delay. Because we'd stripped out the fixed overhead and rebuilt around the walk-in guest, reopening was an operational decision — not a financial one. Revenge tourism arrived. We were the only one ready.

2023 · Breakout · Two years on top

Revenue surges 5.3× year-on-year

First fully profitable post-pivot year. 32.5% net margin. For two consecutive years after COVID, E-O-D was middle India's preferred active entertainment destination. Novelty on our side — aspirational activities that most of our audience had never experienced, at prices that felt almost unreasonably affordable in a moment when the masses were still cautious about spending. ₹299 had trained the market. The market was now hungry. And we were the only neighbourhood park ready to serve it.

2024 · Investment year

New attractions at EMV; agent network scales

8-player Laser Tag. Indoor Kids Playground expansion. Margins compress temporarily. 150+ travel agents now active.

Early 2025 · Park Two

DME opens on the Delhi–Meerut Expressway

Indoor format. Highway location. Family audience. A different archetype — same brand. Opened with a partial activity stack: outdoor zone not yet launched, indoor equipment pending full commissioning. FY26-27 is the first year the complete product operates.

June 2025 · Park Three handover

Late August 2025 · EAC opens

Soft launch at Agra Chaupati

Zipline, Roller Coaster, Go Kart, and Graffiti Ride installed. Rental commences September. Stabilised operations with full systems from October. Agra's first quality active entertainment destination.

November 2025 · Park Four

ESP opens at Subhash Park

A premium neighbourhood public park under ADA concession. The brand completes its second city.

FY25-26 · Scale

All-time high revenue at ₹16.29 Cr

Four parks. Two cities. One team. Template proven and replicating — DME completes its first full year, EAC stabilises over ~6 months, ESP opens its first 4–5 months. Consolidated margins compressed during ramp-up; EMV mature benchmark remains 30%+ net.

How We Make Money

Six revenue streams. No single point of dependence.

Most amusement parks in India earn 80–90% from gate tickets alone. E-O-D has deliberately built a diversified model across six streams — each with its own distribution channel, pricing logic, and growth trajectory.

01

Walk-in Gate

The core. Combo tickets at counter or online. Priced for frequency: ₹200 at EAC, ₹500–600 weekday at EMV. High volume, low friction, daily footfall. Driven by content and UGC, not a sales team.

02

Schools · Travel Agents

10–20 in 2022 → 150+ active agents by FY25-26. Commission built into the package. Self-funding distribution. Currently EMV-primary; EAC and DME school activation starting FY26-27. ₹2.5 Cr target at EMV alone in FY26-27.

03

Corporate & Groups

Inbound leads generated digitally, closed by the same variable-pay off-site salesperson who handles schools. Zero fixed cost. Scales with demand. Birthday packages and corporate day-outs are an active and growing sub-segment.

04

F&B

EMV: Dhinchak Dhaba — full restaurant and buffet on-site. EAC: 30-shop food court transitioning to FOFO (Aug–Nov 2026). ₹1.5 Cr target at EMV — includes restaurant revenue plus events-driven F&B spend.

05

Events

Dandiya Night. Christmas Carnival. Independence Day Mela. Summer Mela. Ticketed; drives off-peak footfall and press visibility. ₹1.5 Cr target at EMV.

06

Annual Pass

Recurring loyal visits — drives repeat frequency and predictable recurring revenue across all parks. Separate from transactional online ticketing.

07

Online Ticketing

Own website (eodparks.com) + BookMyShow. Currently ~1–2% of total sales — an active focus area for FY26-27. Combined online target ₹1.5 Cr at EMV.

The Moat

What stops someone from copying this tomorrow?

Five reasons a new entrant — regardless of capital — cannot replicate this in three years.

01

Government land. No one else can have it.

EMV: DTTDC concession on DDA land at Sanjay Lake. EAC + ESP: ADA concessions in Agra. These are government grants, not negotiable leases. No prime urban park land of this kind is available in central Delhi. A new entrant cannot replicate this location at any price. Zero land cost = permanently superior unit economics.

02

Ten years of brand trust in a trust-deficit market

Safety in amusement is earned, not assumed. Zero bodily injury claims in ten years. 3,000+ Google reviews. A decade of word-of-mouth in East Delhi and Agra. Cannot be bought. A new entrant starts from zero.

03

A distribution network that took years to build

150+ active travel agents on variable commission, selling school packages specifically to E-O-D. Sticky, self-sustaining, three years to build from 10–20 agents. They will not switch to an unproven entrant — and the network compounds with every new park added.

04

Operational knowledge at this ARPU

Running a ₹200–600 ARPU park at the margins EMV demonstrated in its mature year — 32.5% net — is not obvious. Activity mix, combo pricing architecture, staff ratios, F&B model, safety protocols — institutional knowledge accumulated over a decade. Capital can build the rides. It cannot buy ten years of knowing what works.

05

Government relationship capital — the pipeline moat

DTTDC. ADA. Active liaison with GDA, DDA, BDA, Haryana Tourism, Delhi Tourism, UP Tourism. Swadesh Darshan 2.0 stakeholder positioning across four states. A decade of operating credibility with government bodies. When a new government concession opens in North India, E-O-D is already in the room.

You cannot replicate our locations, our trust, our agent network, our operating knowledge, or our government relationships — regardless of how much capital you raise.
